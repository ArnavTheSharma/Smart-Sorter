Waste Disposal Guide

Recyclable:

  • Paper: Newspaper, office paper, magazines, cardboard (flatten boxes).
  • Plastic bottles and containers: Check the recycling number (usually inside a triangle):
    • 1 (PET/PETE): Common in water and soda bottles. Widely recyclable.
    • 2 (HDPE): Milk jugs, detergent bottles. Highly recyclable.
    • 3 (PVC): Pipes, some clear food packaging. Less commonly recycled.
    • 4 (LDPE): Grocery bags, squeezable bottles. Often recycled through special programs.
    • 5 (PP): Yogurt cups, bottle caps. Recyclable in some areas.
    • 6 (PS): Styrofoam, disposable cups. Usually not accepted curbside.
    • 7 (Other): Mixed plastics. Check local recycling guidelines.
  • Metal cans: Aluminum soda cans, tin cans for food. Rinse before recycling.
  • Glass jars: Food and beverage containers. Remove lids, rinse clean.

Compostable:

  • Food scraps: fruit peels, vegetable scraps, leftover food.
  • Tea bags and coffee grounds: remove staples or packaging if any.
  • Plant matter: leaves, small branches, flowers.

Trash:

  • Styrofoam: cups, trays, packaging peanuts.
  • Chip bags, candy wrappers, plastic wrap: usually made from mixed materials.
  • Contaminated containers: takeout boxes with grease or food residue.

Tips for Proper Sorting:

  • Rinse containers to remove food residue to avoid contamination.

  • Separate different materials if possible (plastic lids from glass jars, cardboard liners from pizza boxes).
  • When in doubt, compost biodegradable items or throw non-recyclable trash in the bin.
